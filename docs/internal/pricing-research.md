# Pricing + ESA Expensability Research

_Generated 2026-05-25. Decision basis for v0.6.0 launch pricing._

## TL;DR

- **Pricing:** Hold at $4.99/mo, $39.99/yr. Correctly anchored on Cozi Gold ($39/yr).
- **ESA expensability:** NO across FES-UA / FES-EO / PEP. Position upfront in marketing as a trust signal, not a workaround.
- **Future bump option:** $49/yr is the safest if we ever raise. Stay under Day One Silver ($49.99).
- **Do NOT chase** the YNAB/Monarch tier ($95-109/yr) — those manage all of family finance; ScholarKeep is single-vertical.

## ESA expensability verdicts

Legal frame across all three: scholarship funds pay for instructional materials/services for the **student**. ScholarKeep is a parent administrative tool, same category as TurboTax / YNAB.

| Program | Probability of reimbursement | Most plausible denial reason |
|---|---|---|
| FES-UA | LOW | "Administrative tool for parent, not instructional material for student" / provider not registered |
| FES-EO / FTC | LOW | Outside eligible categories — not student-facing |
| PEP | LOW-MEDIUM | Parent-side administrative tool; analogous to TurboTax/YNAB, neither of which is reimbursed |

**No precedent** for tax-prep (TurboTax) or budgeting (YNAB, Monarch) software being reimbursed under any of the three programs. ScholarKeep is in the same category.

## Strategic framing — why explicit honesty is the move

Three failure modes if we imply reimbursability:
1. **Trust collapse** when a parent submits and gets denied
2. **Regulator friction** with Step Up / AAA / FCSUA
3. **Wrong positioning** — competes with parents' free time, not their scholarship budget

Instead, lead with:
> "Pay with your own card, not your scholarship — this app isn't a scholarship-eligible expense, and I'd rather be honest about that than try to sell you a workaround."

This positions ScholarKeep as eligibility-discipline-tool-that-practices-what-it-preaches. Stronger than any reimbursability claim could ever be.

## Caveat

Step Up's official Purchasing Guide PDFs are image-based; verdicts above rely on public summaries, the FES-UA AAA Handbook, and prior years' language. Re-verify the "directly benefits the student" / "administrative tool" language against the live PDFs and ideally a Step Up support call (877-735-7837) before publishing any reimbursability claim.
