# Sarah's Audit: ScholarKeep through a real FL homeschool mom's eyes

_Run through Sarah Whitlock (see persona-sarah.md). It's 9:42pm Wednesday. Kids are asleep. David's watching basketball. She has 40 minutes before bed._

---

## TOUCHPOINT 1: She sees the FB post

**What she sees** (Version 2, in a thread in the "Homeschool Free and Affordable" Facebook group):

> Florida homeschool moms — a few of us dads built an iPhone app to handle ESA scholarship receipts and submissions because we were tired of doing it on paper every July. Scans receipts, tells you what's eligible right then citing the official guide, tracks your claims, reminds you about deadlines. Local-only, no servers. Works for FES-UA, FES-EO, and PEP.
>
> Looking for 5 beta testers. DM me if you want the link.

**Sarah's reaction:**

> "Ok, FL — that's me. Homeschool dads? Sure, I'll allow it. Receipts and submissions, yes that's the problem. Cites the official guide — hmm, ok, that's specific enough to be real. Local-only no servers — wait, that's actually good. Free beta, just need to DM. Five spots."
>
> "But — *who* are you? I don't see a name. Just 'a few of us dads.' I'd want to know which co-op you're in or something. And how do I know you're actually in Florida and not some startup in California pretending?"

**Severity: 🟡** — The post is good but lacks a credibility anchor. Sarah will probably scroll past unless someone she knows comments.

**Fixes:**
- **Add a name at the end:** "— Carlos in Lutz, FES-UA dad" (geographic + program signal)
- **Add a credibility tell:** "PS happy to show my Step Up parent portal screenshot if you don't believe I'm actually doing this 😅"
- **Optional but powerful:** "My wife is currently doing the July sort while I write this. We needed this."

## TOUCHPOINT 2: She clicks the link / hears scholarkeep.app

**What she sees** (skyet4545.github.io/ScholarKeep — current live state):

1. The dark by-line pill: "From homeschool families, to homeschool families" — *good, exactly the warm collective signal*
2. The H1: "The Florida ESA app made for **how you already work.**" — *neutral, slightly abstract*
3. The lede: "Scan a receipt at 9pm after the kids are in bed..." — *🎯 BULLSEYE. That's her, right now.*
4. The iPhone mockup on the right with balance + claims + activity
5. Trust pill: "Everything stays on your phone"
6. "Join the beta →" CTA

**Sarah's reaction:**

> "9pm after the kids are in bed — they get it. Ok. *Reads more.* No fake stock photos. Real-looking iPhone mockup. Pricing — wait, where's the pricing? Oh, I have to scroll. Let me scroll."

She scrolls. Sees the founder quote.

> "'We're two homeschool dads in Florida — but every family knows the truth: our wives do 95% of the receipts.' Hahaha — ok, that's honest. 'They keep us honest.' Yeah, I bet they do. Carlos and Ramsey. Real names. Two avatars. I'll keep reading."

Then the 5-axis differentiator.

> "Five things that make this different. Ok. *Native iOS* — not web. *On-device privacy* — they say it more than once, that's important. *$39.99 not $144* — ok, they're comparing themselves to something. I bet they mean HeyPeppy. Smart not to name them. *All three programs* — that's a real thing for me, June will be on PEP, Maya is on UA. *AI helps, you decide* — that's exactly the line. I don't want AI doing my $400 curriculum bundle wrong."

She scrolls to "Three things it does."

> "Scan in seconds — sure, every app says that. *Can I buy this?* — that's the actual question I have at Target. Show me. *Submission-ready* — yes, July 31 is the worst week of my year."

She lands on the privacy block (dark card).

> "Your kid's records, your phone. ✓ no analytics ✓ no servers we operate ✓ not affiliated with Step Up — *that's the line that matters most.* If you'd buried this I would have bounced."

She gets to pricing.

> "$0 free. $39.99/yr Pro. Same as Cozi. I can do that. Wait — 'pay with your own card — this app isn't a scholarship-eligible expense.' *That's interesting.* They told me upfront I can't expense it. That's the most trustworthy thing anyone selling me software has ever said."

She skims FAQ.

> "Can I use my scholarship — no, and I want to be explicit. *Good.* Why iPhone only — solo project. *Reasonable.* Android version — they're being honest about being small. Fine."

She gets to the beta block.

> "Email me for the TestFlight link. Hmm. I'd rather just... tap a button? I don't really want to send an email."

**Severity by section:**
- Hero: 🟢 Strong
- Founder quote: 🟢 Strong
- 5-axis differentiator: 🟢 Strong (Sarah specifically noticed the price comparison)
- Use cases: 🟡 OK but generic ("scan in seconds" is fluff she's heard before)
- Privacy block: 🟢 Strong
- Pricing: 🟢 Strong (the "pay with your own card" line was the trust peak)
- FAQ: 🟢 Good
- Beta CTA: 🟡 — `mailto:` is friction. Sarah would prefer a form OR a TestFlight public link

**Fixes:**
- **🟡 Use cases section:** rewrite from generic to specific. "Scan receipts in seconds" → "Snap your stack of Target receipts in 30 seconds" — concrete. Sarah-language.
- **🟡 Beta CTA:** swap `mailto:` for a TestFlight public link OR a Tally/Google Form — moms in 2026 don't love opening Mail
- **🟢 Add a single testimonial slot** once you have one — even one mom quote 5x the trust of the founder quote
- **🟢 Above the founder quote, consider adding:** "Used by [N] FL families on FES-UA, FES-EO, and PEP" — even N=3 builds trust once it's real

## TOUCHPOINT 3: She gets the TestFlight invite

David hands her his phone (she's already on the iPhone Settings page checking screen time). She doesn't have time tonight. She opens TestFlight at 10pm Thursday.

**What she sees:** Standard TestFlight install. App icon (terracotta graduation cap). "Test Notes" section showing "v0.6.2 build 12" — and...

**Sarah's reaction:**

> "What did they actually change in this version? The What's New is empty. That's not great. Real software ships with notes."

**Severity: 🟡** — Empty release notes look amateur. Niche-but-beloved apps (Mela, NetNewsWire) win on visible, human-voice changelog.

**Fix:**
- **TestFlight What's New** should read: "v0.6.2 (May 25) — Added one-tap account deletion, polished accessibility labels, and snuck in our Apple privacy manifest. Tap 'Send feedback' in Settings if anything looks broken. — Carlos & Ramsey"

## TOUCHPOINT 4: She launches the app

She taps the icon. Sees the Welcome screen.

**What she sees:**
- Big circular terracotta graduation-cap icon
- "Welcome to" (small) + "ScholarKeep" (38pt bold)
- "A private companion for Florida ESA scholarship parents — so you never lose a receipt or miss a deadline."
- 🔒 pill: "Everything stays on your phone"
- "Get started" button

**Sarah's reaction:**

> "Oh, that's pretty. Warm. The cream background is nice — most apps are stark white. Get started."

She taps. **Sign in with Apple gate appears.**

> "Wait — I have to sign in first? I just want to look around. This is the part I hate."

**Severity: 🔴** — This is the Bear-pattern miss from the audit. Sarah wants to *see* the app before committing identity. Forcing Sign in with Apple on screen 1 is a 2025-era pattern; 2026-era apps defer auth until value lands.

**Fix:**
- **Defer Sign in with Apple** until either (a) the user enables iCloud sync, or (b) they finish onboarding. The first 5 minutes of the app should be available anonymous.

She sighs and taps Sign in with Apple anyway.

**How it works screen:**

> "Three benefit cards. Scan receipts in seconds. Get a verdict instantly. Stay submission-ready. *Yes, those are the three things I want. Next.*"

**Add Your First Student:**

> "First name or nickname. Maya. Program — let me pick FES-UA. *Oh, 'Not sure which one?' link.* That's helpful for someone newer than me. *Skip.* Next."

**Disclaimer screen:**

> "Two fact cards. 'We're not Step Up, AAA, or the state.' *Good.* 'Verdicts are educated estimates.' *Good — they're managing my expectations.* Continue."

**Preferences:**

> "Face ID — yes. Reminders — yes. iCloud backup — *hmm, it says 'sync ships later.'* So... it's not actually backing up? What if I lose my phone tomorrow? I just spent 10 minutes putting Maya's info in."

**Severity: 🔴** — This is the critical risk the audit already flagged. The iCloud toggle is misleading. Sarah will absolutely worry about data loss.

**Fix:**
- **Either ship real CloudKit sync** OR **remove the toggle entirely** and replace with text: "iCloud sync coming in v0.7. For now, your data lives on this device only — if you switch phones, you'll start fresh."

She taps Finish.

- 3 tiles: 0 claims, 0 receipts, no upcoming deadlines
- No activity feed (empty)
- No key dates (no data yet)

**Sarah's reaction:**

> "Empty. Ok, makes sense. But I don't know what to do next. There's a + in the top right? Let me tap that. Oh — scan with camera, pick from library. Right, I need to add a receipt."

**Severity: 🟡** — The first-time empty state is functional but not guiding. Things 3's "see it filled in" pattern would help.

**Fix:**
- **First-time empty state should be a coach card:** "Maya's dashboard will show her balance, recent receipts, and deadlines here. Tap **+** to add your first receipt — or share one from Mail."

She taps the + and selects "Pick from library." Picks a screenshot of an Office Depot receipt she has from last week.

**Scan flow:**

She watches the OCR happen. Vision parses it. She lands on the Review screen.

> "$84.97. Office Depot. May 18. *Category — Curriculum.* That's right. *Verdict — Likely Eligible* with the actual section quoted: 'Curriculum and instructional materials are eligible under FES-UA Section 4.2.' *Wait, that's actually what I'd write on the receipt myself.* This is the first time an app has shown me the rule alongside the verdict. I'll save this."

**Severity: 🟢** — This is the moment Sarah converts. The cited verdict from the actual Purchasing Guide is the killer feature. Sarah just told you why she'd pay $39.

A week in. Another mom in her FB group asks: "@Sarah you mentioned ScholarKeep — is it actually any good?"

**Sarah's reply (the honest version):**

> "Yeah, I've been using it for a week. The thing that won me over was that when I check a purchase, it cites the actual line of the Purchasing Guide — so I trust the answer. The receipt scanner is fast. And I love that I can share from Mail to it directly.
>
> Only complaint: the iCloud backup toggle is confusing, it's not actually backing up yet. And it makes you sign in with Apple before you can poke around. But it's $0 right now (and $40/year if I want PDFs), so I'm in. The dad who built it (Carlos, in Lutz) is responsive when I send feedback through the app."

**This is the recommendation that matters most.** Everything we've built either earns this paragraph or doesn't. Today, it ALMOST earns it — but the two complaints would prevent her from being a strong advocate.
