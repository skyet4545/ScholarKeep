## Where to post

1. **"Florida Homeschoolers" Facebook group** (largest umbrella group)
2. **"FES-UA Parents Florida" Facebook group** (the audience that needs this most — therapy + curriculum heavy)
3. **"PEP Florida Homeschool" Facebook group** (homeschool-specific, eligible item rules different)
4. **"Step Up For Students Family" Facebook group** (official-adjacent, larger audience)
5. **FPEA discussion forum + member directory** (florida.edu network)
6. **Local FL homeschool co-op leader chats** (text or WhatsApp — high trust)
7. **r/HomeschoolRecovery** (use FL flair, mention bootstrapped, no commercial framing)

## What NOT to do

- Don't post in groups where you're not already a member or known — moderators will flag self-promo.
- Don't pitch on Step Up's official channels (FB, email) — they're not affiliated with the app and the disclaimer matters.
- Don't lead with the Pro subscription. The beta is free. Mention pricing only if asked.
- Don't claim eligibility verdicts are official. The phrase is "based on the official guide" not "official."

## Feedback collection

For each tester, after they've used the app for ~5 days, send a single DM:

> Hey [Name] — quick check-in. What's the ONE thing that's annoyed you most about ScholarKeep so far? And the ONE thing you'd add if you could?

Two open questions. No surveys. Real signal.

## Track them

Spreadsheet or Notion:
- Tester name | FL county | Program (UA/EO/PEP) | Number of kids | TestFlight invite sent | First 5 days feedback | What they want next

10 testers = enough signal to ship v1.0 confidently.
