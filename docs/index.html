Common questions

Is this affiliated with Step Up For Students, AAA, or the state?

No, and I want to be loud about that. ScholarKeep is an independent personal-records app. It doesn't connect to EMA, SMP, MyScholarShop, Tipalti, or any official portal. You still submit claims through the official channels yourself. Verdicts here are estimates based on the published Purchasing Guides — always confirm against the official guide before big purchases.

Does it work for FES-EO and PEP, not just FES-UA?

Yes. The eligibility ruleset covers FES-UA, FES-EO, and PEP — each program has its own category list and rules baked in. If you have kids on different programs, ScholarKeep handles them separately on the same device.

What about PEP — do you handle the SLP rule?

Yes. When you add a PEP student, you set the SLP-approved date. Anything purchased before that date is permanently ineligible under PEP rules, and ScholarKeep will mark it so you don't waste a submission on it.

What if the Purchasing Guide changes mid-year?

The ruleset is versioned and updates over-the-air. When the FLDOE / Step Up publishes a new guide, I update the ruleset and your app pulls the latest version. You can see the version + last-updated date in Settings.

Why iPhone only? Android version?

Solo project, iPhone is what I know. If there's strong demand from beta testers I'll look at an Android build later. For now: iPhone only.

What does it cost?

Free for everything most families need: eligibility verdicts, receipt scanning, claim tracking, reminders, recurring tasks. Pro is $4.99/month or $39.99/year and adds PDF submission packages, CSV exports, iCloud backup, and year-end reports. Family Sharing covers your whole household on one Pro.

For comparison: Cozi Gold (family calendar) is $39/year; Day One (journaling) is $50/year. ScholarKeep at $39.99 is the same mental slot, but for the part of homeschool that actually has money on the line.

Can I use my FES-UA / PEP / FES-EO scholarship to pay for ScholarKeep?

No — and I want to be explicit about that. The Purchasing Guides cover instructional materials and services for your student. ScholarKeep is a parent administrative tool, same category as TurboTax or YNAB. Step Up doesn't reimburse those, and they wouldn't reimburse ScholarKeep either.

Pay for ScholarKeep with your own card, not your scholarship. I hold this app to the same eligibility rules it helps you check. If anyone tells you otherwise, they're misreading the guide.

Can I trust my data won't end up somewhere?

Yes. There are no servers — I literally don't operate any. No third-party analytics SDKs (no Firebase, Mixpanel, Google Analytics, nothing). Sign in with Apple is used only to lock the app to your Apple ID — the identifier stays on your device. If you turn on iCloud backup, your records sync through your iCloud account, not mine.
